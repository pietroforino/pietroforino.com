MUFOCO

Client
Museum di Fotografia Contemporanea
Role
Interaction Designer and frontend developer @ Dotdotdot
Deliverables
Create the museum's new digital touchpoints with a focus on accessibility.

© Andrea Martiradonna


On the occasion of the museum's 20th anniversary, Mufoco-the only public museum in Italy dedicated to contemporary photography, located near Milan-has commissioned Dotdotdot to design and implement the new digital touchpoints. Mufoco has a collection of more than 2 million works, which for obvious issues of space has never been given the proper space. Thanks to our work, these works have instead found a new way to be explored.

© Andrea Martiradonna


For Mufoco, we made a structure consisting of 6 LED arrays, to be placed outside, while once you enter you can immediately find the first interactive touchpoint. With 6 screens and a touchscreen frame, users (up to 3 at a time) can explore the museum's endless collection, starting with a photograph and exploring the archive by authors, dates, genres and themes.
Among the project's biggest challenges was precisely creating a Web experience capable of processing such a large number of images, both from a technical and a UX and UI perspective.

© Andrea Martiradonna
